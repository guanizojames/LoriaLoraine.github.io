    • Foods

      Food is an essential part of every culture, connecting us with others during our travels and leaving us with unforgettable memories. With countless cuisines around the world, let me guide you on a delicious foodie adventure, one bite at a time!

      I've loved cooking since I was 16 and living on my own. What started as a necessity soon turned into a passion for planning meals and discovering new recipes.

      Living independently since 2020 has made cooking even more enjoyable, as I'm constantly exposed to diverse cuisines. My fascination with creating authentic dishes has only grown over time. There's something magical about making food from scratch, especially when it's from another culture!â™¥

      Foods in Oriental Mindoro:

      Kinilaw na Tanguige: A traditional Filipino ceviche made with fresh tuna marinated in vinegar, calamansi juice, onions, and chili peppers.

      Inubaran: A local dish featuring banana blossoms cooked with coconut milk, shrimp, and spices, resulting in a creamy and flavorful stew.

      Foods in Antipolo:

      Kesong Puti: Fresh, soft cheese made from carabao's milk, perfect for pairing with bread or as a snack on its own.

      Suman sa Lihiya: Sticky rice cakes wrapped in banana leaves and cooked with lye water, creating a unique flavor and texture.

      Foods in Metro Manila:

      Adobo: The quintessential Filipino dish of meat (chicken, pork, or even seafood) marinated in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and spices, then simmered until tender.

      Pancit Palabok: Thin rice noodles topped with a rich shrimp sauce, crushed chicharron (pork rinds), hard-boiled eggs, shrimp, and green onions.

      Foods in Batangas:

      Batangas Lomi: A thick noodle soup with a flavorful broth made from pork, chicken, and shrimp, garnished with slices of pork, liver, and boiled egg.

      Bulalo: A hearty beef soup made from bone marrow, tender beef shanks, corn, and vegetables, simmered until the flavors meld together perfectly.

      Foods in Quezon City:

      Halo-Halo: A beloved Filipino dessert consisting of shaved ice, various sweet ingredients such as beans, jellies, fruits, and topped with leche flan (caramel custard) and ice cream.

      Sisig: A sizzling dish made from chopped pig's face and ears, seasoned with calamansi, onions, and chili peppers, typically served on a hot plate.
